<p><p>Dental plaster is commonly categorized into five types based on their varying levels of strength and setting time. Type I is the weakest and used for study models, while Type II is slightly stronger and used for pouring preliminary impressions. Type III has higher strength and is used for working casts. Type IV is even stronger and used for orthodontic models, and Type V is the strongest and used for crown and bridge models. Each type serves a specific purpose in dental applications.</p></p>
