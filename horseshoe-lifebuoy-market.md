<p><p>Horseshoe lifebuoys come in two main types: inherent and inflatable. Inherent type lifebuoys are solid, usually made of foam or plastic, and are designed to provide buoyancy without any need for inflation. Inflatable type lifebuoys, on the other hand, are made of airtight material that can be inflated when needed. Both types are used for water safety and rescue purposes, but inflatable lifebuoys are preferred in situations where storage space is limited or rapid deployment is required.</p></p>
