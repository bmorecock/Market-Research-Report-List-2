<p><p>Diffraction gratings are optical elements used to disperse light into its component wavelengths. There are two main types of diffraction gratings: holographic gratings and ruled gratings. Holographic gratings are created using a holographic interference pattern, offering high diffraction efficiency and precise control over groove spacing. Ruled gratings are produced by physically ruling grooves onto a reflective surface, providing lower cost alternatives with broader wavelength ranges. Both types of diffraction gratings are essential in various applications including spectroscopy, telecommunications, and laser systems.</p></p>
