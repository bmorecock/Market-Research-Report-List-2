<p><p>Hydrogenated cottonseed oil comes in two main types: partially hydrogenated and fully hydrogenated. Partially hydrogenated oil is created through a process that adds hydrogen to the oil, resulting in a semi-solid texture. Fully hydrogenated oil, on the other hand, undergoes a complete hydrogenation process, turning it solid at room temperature. These different types of hydrogenation affect the oil's stability, texture, and shelf life, making them suitable for various applications in the food industry.</p></p>
