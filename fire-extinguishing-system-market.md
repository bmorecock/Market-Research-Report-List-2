<p><p>Fire extinguishing systems can be broadly categorized into two types: automatic and non-automatic systems. </p><p>Automatic fire extinguishing systems are designed to detect and extinguish fires without human intervention. These systems typically include smoke detectors, heat sensors, and automatic sprinklers that activate when a fire is detected.</p><p>Non-automatic fire extinguishing systems, on the other hand, rely on human intervention to operate. These systems include portable fire extinguishers, fire blankets, and fire hoses that must be manually activated to extinguish fires.</p></p>
