<p><p>Methacrylic Acid (MAA) is primarily produced using two different methods: the Acetone Cyanohydrin Method and the Isobutylene Oxidation Method. The Acetone Cyanohydrin Method involves the reaction of acetone cyanohydrin with sulfuric acid, while the Isobutylene Oxidation Method involves the oxidation of isobutylene with air or oxygen. These two methods result in the production of MAA, which is widely used in the production of numerous products such as coatings, adhesives, and plastics.</p></p>
