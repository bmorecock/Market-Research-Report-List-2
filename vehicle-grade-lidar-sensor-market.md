<p><p>Mechanical Lidar systems use rotating mirrors to steer the laser beams and are typically bulky, expensive, and have moving parts that can wear out over time. Solid State Lidar systems, on the other hand, have no moving parts and are more compact, lightweight, and cost-effective. Solid State Lidar technology is rapidly advancing, offering higher reliability and better performance compared to mechanical Lidar systems. This has led to an increasing demand for Solid State Lidar sensors in various industries such as automotive, robotics, and smart infrastructure.</p></p>
