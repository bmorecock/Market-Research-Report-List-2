<p><p>Clean room air filters remove dust, particles, and contaminants from the air in controlled environments such as hospitals, laboratories, and manufacturing facilities. The two main types of filters are HEPA (High Efficiency Particulate Air) and ULPA (Ultra Low Particulate Air) filters. HEPA filters are designed to remove 99.97% of particles that are 0.3 microns in size, while ULPA filters can remove particles as small as 0.12 microns. Both types of filters help maintain clean and sterile environments by trapping harmful particles in the air.</p></p>
