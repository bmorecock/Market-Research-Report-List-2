Xylanase derived from animal sources is typically derived from the pancreas or saliva of animals. Plant-origin xylanase is extracted from plants such as wheat or barley. Microbial-source xylanase is produced by microorganisms such as bacteria or fungi through fermentation processes. Each type of xylanase has unique characteristics and may be preferred based on factors such as cost, functionality, and source availability.</p></p>
