<p><p>Solid Electrolyte Supercapacitors consist of two main types - Double Layer and Pseudocapacitors. Double Layer capacitors store energy through the physical separation of ions at the electrolyte-electrode interface, allowing for quick charge and discharge cycles. Pseudocapacitors, on the other hand, store energy through fast redox reactions at the electrode surface, offering higher energy densities compared to Double Layer capacitors. Both types are gaining popularity in the market for their high power density, long cycle life, and enhanced safety features.</p></p>
